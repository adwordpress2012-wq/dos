# Directive OS Outreach Playbook

The PDF at `exports/directive-os-outreach-playbook.pdf` is the canonical reference. Regenerate via `node exports/generate_outreach_pdf.js` after edits.

## Tone Rules
- **Warm, casual, professional** — agent-to-agent, never salesy
- Always sign off with first name (e.g. "Cheers, Jayson")
- Max 1 emoji per message
- Never send 2 messages in 24 hours

## 4-Touch Schedule (applies to all channels)
| Touch | Day | Purpose |
|-------|-----|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Intro |
| 2 | Day 2-3 | Follow-up |
| 3 | Day 5 | Soft close |
| 4 | Day 7-10 | Final note |

## Email Sequence

### Email 1 — Intro
**Subject:** Quick hello from one agent to another
> Hi [Name],
>
> Hope you're having a good week. I'm [Your Name] — I work in real estate too, and lately I've been helping a few agencies set up smarter ways to handle calls and leads after hours.
>
> Thought I'd reach out and say hi. Would you be open to a quick chat at some point? Happy to work around whatever suits you.
>
> Cheers,
> [Your Name]

### Email 2 — Follow-up
**Subject:** Just bumping this up
> Hi [Name],
>
> Just popping back in case my last note got lost in the inbox.
>
> Is there a time that works better for a quick chat? Even a 5-minute call is fine if you're flat out.
>
> Cheers, [Your Name]

### Email 3 — Soft close
**Subject:** All good if not the right time
> Hi [Name],
>
> Totally understand things get busy. If now isn't ideal, no stress — just let me know if I should circle back later, or if there's someone else on the team I should chat to instead.
>
> Cheers, [Your Name]

### Email 4 — Final touch
**Subject:** Last one from me
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'll leave it here for now. If it's easier than a call, I can send through a quick overview — just say the word.
>
> Either way, all the best with the listings.
>
> Cheers, [Your Name]

## SMS Sequence

1. **Intro:** `Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] — fellow agent. Been helping a few agencies sort out after-hours calls and lead capture. Worth a quick chat? No pressure either way 👍`
2. **Follow-up (Day 2):** `Hey [Name], just bumping this in case you missed it. Even a 5-min chat works if you're flat out — let me know what suits.`
3. **Soft close (Day 4-5):** `All good if now's not the right time [Name]. Want me to circle back later, or is there someone else on your team I should chat to?`
4. **Final (Day 7):** `Last one from me [Name] 🙂 Happy to send a quick overview instead of a call if easier — just say the word. All the best with the listings.`

## WhatsApp Sequence

1. **Intro:** `Hey [Name], hope you're going well 👋 It's [Your Name] — I'm in real estate myself. Been working on something that helps agencies pick up after-hours calls and capture leads automatically (basically a virtual receptionist trained for property enquiries). Thought I'd reach out and see if it's worth a quick chat. No sales pitch — just a chat between agents. Let me know what suits 🙂`
2. **Follow-up (Day 2-3):** `Hey [Name], just floating this back up in case it slipped through. Happy to keep it super quick — even 5 mins between appointments works. Would later this week suit?`
3. **Soft close (Day 4-5):** `All good if the timing's off [Name] 👍 Want me to circle back in a few weeks, or is there someone else at the office I should have a chat to?`
4. **Final (Day 7-10):** `Hey [Name], I'll leave it here for now. If easier, I can flick through a quick 1-pager so you can have a look in your own time — just let me know. Either way, all the best with the listings 🏡`

## Channel Strategy
- **Email first** → call same day if they open or click
- **No open in 48h** → SMS or WhatsApp
- **4 touches no reply** → move on, circle back in 60-90 days

## Best Send Times (AEST)
- **Days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Times:** 9-11am or 2-4pm
- **Avoid:** Monday, Friday afternoon, weekends

## Channel Tips
- **WhatsApp:** Use head-shot profile photo, set About to "Real estate + AI tools". Voice notes (15-20s) get 2-3x more replies than text.
- **SMS:** Always sign off with first name so they know it's a person.
- **Email:** Keep subject lines lowercase/casual — feels more personal than a marketing blast.
